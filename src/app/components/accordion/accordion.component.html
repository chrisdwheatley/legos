accordion

The accordion component is very simple with a few enhancements that you can add depending on what you want to use it for. By default, the accordion is in the closed state but with a slight modification, you can switch it to be open by default. The accordion will take up the entire space of its parent container or you can add a max-width property to the wrapper element. There are also additional modification styles you can add. See below for a list of them.

Basic

The basic accordion has squared corners and white background.

Panel

To make your accordion look like a panel add --panel to the accordion class. That's it!

By changing your accordion to a panel:

  • It will round the edges
  • It adds a background color for the accordion title
  • It adds uniform padding to the accordion contents

  <div class="accordion--panel">

    </div>

    </p>
  </div>
</div>

Other Modifications

  • Add secondary text on the accordion title
  • Add icons inside the accordion title
  • Round the edges of the accordion (can't see unless you have a skin on the basic version or background color behind it)
  • Add skin color

Skins

There are four skin colors you can add to an accordion. They change the color, border-color, background-color, and arrow color. You have to put the skin on the accordion element, inside the .accordion__wrapper or it will not work. This is only useful for the .accordion--panel because the default accordion was made to look like it was floating. The two accordions have very different use cases.
