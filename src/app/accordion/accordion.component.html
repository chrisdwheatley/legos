accordion

Our base accordion is very simple with a few enhancements that you can add depending on what you want to use it for. The accordion has a wrapper, meaning you can add several accordions to one containers for them to be grouped together. By default the accordion is in the closed state but with a slight modification you can switch it to open by defaul. They will take up the entire space of their parent container or you can add a max-width property to the wrapper element. There are also additional modification styles you can add on. I'll walk you through them below.

Basic

The basic accordion has squared corners and has a white background.

Panel

To make your accordions look like panels just add these classes onto the base classess. Add --panel to the accordion class. That's it!

By changing your accordion to a panel it:

  • rounds the edges
  • adds a background color for the title
  • adds uniform padding to the accordion contents

<div class="accordion__wrapper">
  <div class="accordion--panel">
    <input type="checkbox" checked>
    <i></i>
    <div class="accordion__title">
      Header 2
    </span>
    </div>
    <p class="accordion__container">
      <span>This is something in the container</span>
    </p>
  </div>
</div>
Other Modifications
  • Add secondary text on title
  • Add icons inside the title
  • Round the edged of the accordion (can't see unless you have a skin on the basic version or a background color behind it)
  • Add skin colors

Skins

We provide four different types of skins. They transform color, border-color, background-color, and arrow color. You have to put the skin color with the accordion class, inside the accordion__wrapper or it will not work. This only really useful for the accordion--panel because the default accordion was made to look like it was floating. The two different accordions have very different use cases.

Example: the floating navigation on the left side of the screen.
