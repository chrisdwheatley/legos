alerts

Alerts are messages that are brought on by different types of user actions. There are two different types of alerts. Use the main alert class on your container.

Attributes: role="alert".

Global Alerts

This type of alert gets fixed to the top of the screen and stays until dismissed with the close button. This is what differentiates it from a notification alert that fades on it's own after a certain amount of time.

Demo:

* I would have a demo here but it would just stay fixed to the top and that doesn't seem necessary, as it would just sit at the top of this screen.

Markup:
<div class="alert alert--global alert--danger" role="alert">
  ...
</div>
Notifcation Alerts

This type of alert fades into an area of the viewport brought on by some user interaction. They fade-in and then out after a certain amount of time. For these notifications you can customize their placement by adding your own position property to it. Recommendation: top right.

Demo:
.alert--success
Markup:
<div class="alert alert--notification alert--danger" role="alert">
  ...
</div>
Alert Colors
  • alert--danger
  • alert--success
  • alert--info
  • alert--warning

These colors control the alerts:

  • Backgroud Color
  • Font Color
  • Close Button Color (only for alert--global)
  • Border Color (only for alert--notification)
Demo:
alert--danger
alert--success
alert--info
alert--warning
Markup:
<div class="alert alert--global alert--danger" role="alert">
  <div>Alert text here</div>
  <button type="button" class="alert__close">
    <i class="fas fa-times close" aria-hidden="true"></i>
  </button>
</div>
